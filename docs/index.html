About SuperSearch

What is this?

SuperSearch maps the known superconductor composition space using dimensionality reduction (PCA) on 21,263 materials from the UCI Superconductivity Database. Each point represents a material, positioned by its elemental composition features and colored by critical temperature (Tc).

Complementing GNoME Predictions

Google DeepMind's GNoME predicted 380,000+ stable crystal structures, but predicting stability is different from predicting superconductivity. SuperSearch identifies where experimental coverage is thin — the gaps between known materials that GNoME's candidates could fill.

  • GNoME provides candidate structures
  • SuperSearch shows where to look first
  • Together: targeted synthesis priorities

Guiding Autonomous Lab Prioritization

For an autonomous lab deciding what to synthesize next, composition space coverage matters. The opportunity regions highlighted here represent unexplored neighborhoods adjacent to known high-Tc materials — the highest-value targets for experimental campaigns.

  • Sparse regions → experimental priorities
  • Cluster boundaries → composition phase transitions
  • High-Tc patterns → hypothesis generation

Hypothesis Generation

SuperSearch enables systematic hypothesis generation: "Materials with atomic mass 85–115 and valence ~2.0–2.5 cluster in the high-Tc region. Unexplored compositions in adjacent sparse zones with similar properties are strong synthesis candidates."

Data

21,263 superconductors from the UCI Superconductivity Database (Hamidieh, 2018). Features: mean atomic mass, atomic radius, valence, and 7 other elemental statistics. PCA retains 73.5% variance in 2 components. DBSCAN identifies 3 composition families. 5,000-point sample displayed for performance.
